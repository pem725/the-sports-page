# Mets Buy-or-Sell Watch — 2026 Season

## The Editorial Question

With Clay Holmes on the IL (fractured fibula, weeks-to-months), the Mets' rotation is being held together by Nolan McLean and a thin depth chart behind him. The roster decision point is approaching: **will Mets management treat 2026 as a lost year and pull up minor leaguers for development reps, OR double down at the trade deadline to buy a championship-caliber pitching staff?** Both are defensible. Both have very different implications for 2027 and beyond. The eventual piece tracks the front office's actual choice against what the math says they should have done.

**Update (2026-06-26):** The question has sharpened from "buy or sell" to "sell-off or stay the course." The Mets fired manager Carlos Mendoza on June 26 at 34-47 (last in the NL East) while carrying a $375M+ CBT payroll — second-highest in baseball. POBO David Stearns was retained and is publicly *hedging* on the deadline (wants to "give the team a chance" before deciding). So the "fire sale" is anticipated, not announced. The watch now has a third variable: a new interim manager (Andy Green) and 81 games to either justify buying or confirm selling.

## Decision Points

- **All-Star Break**: Tuesday, July 14, 2026 — first opportunity for a public signal
- **Trade Deadline**: Monday, August 3, 2026 (6pm ET) — the actual buy-or-sell line
- **September 1 roster expansion**: secondary signal of development priorities

## What to Track

For each starting pitcher in the projected rotation:
- IP, ERA, FIP, xFIP, SIERA
- Per-start basis where useful (e.g., catastrophic outings)

For the team:
- W-L trajectory; Pythagorean expected W-L; BaseRuns expected W-L
- Run differential weekly snapshot
- Distance from AL/NL Wild Card 3 line

For the front office signals:
- Minor-league call-ups (especially pitchers)
- Veteran rest / DH-only days suggesting protection
- Public statements from David Stearns
- Trade chatter (always grain of salt)

- Context: This was McLean's start in the projected rotation post-Holmes. One bad start does not a verdict make, but it's the first signal.

### 2026-06-26 — Mets fire manager Carlos Mendoza; sell-off looms
- **Fired Friday, June 26**, the day after a four-game sweep by the Cubs at Citi Field — the series whose six-error nightcap and Dansby Swanson 15-RBI eruption we covered in **Issue #90** (published the morning *before* the firing).
- **Record at firing: 34-47, last in the NL East.**
- **Payroll context: $375M+ CBT — second-highest in MLB behind only the Dodgers.** Second-highest payroll, last place. This is the "spend-a-ton-win-less" thesis with an exclamation point.
- **Andy Green** named interim manager for the final 81 games. Steve Cohen: *"Fans deserve better."*
- **David Stearns (POBO) RETAINED.** Yahoo's framing: the firing "has little to do with him [Mendoza]." The roster reload was Stearns's; the manager was the scapegoat.
- **Deadline signal — Stearns is HEDGING:** said publicly he wants to give the team a chance to get back into the playoff hunt before making deadline decisions. As of 6/26, *not* a declared seller.
- **Sources:** CBS Sports, ESPN, Yahoo Sports, Chicago Sun-Times, US News (all 6/26/2026).
- **Buy-or-sell read:** firing the manager mid-season while retaining the POBO and hedging on the deadline is the move of an org buying *time*, not committing to a direction. The real signal is the next ~3 weeks toward the All-Star Break (7/14). A genuine fire sale would start showing up as veteran-for-prospect trades; watch for the first one.
